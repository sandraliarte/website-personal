Conceptual Thinking

"So far, our concept of space has been associated with the box. It turns out, however, that the storage possibilities that make up the box-space are independent of the thickness of the walls of the box. Cannot this thickness be reduced to zero, without the "space" being lost as a result? The naturalness of such a limiting process is obvious, and now there remains for our thought the space without the box, a self-evident thing, yet it appears to be so unreal if we forget the origin of this concept." Albert Einstein.

Single Line

Due to the beauty of the landscape, I thought to make a minimum and unitary project, where inside and outside could be the same. I imagine a horizontal plane that dilutes the vertical elements, fused better in the landscape.
So, I thought a ribbon that acted of floor and ceiling,a ribbon that bends on itself to create all the spaces, being the only materiality of the project. And, between the planes of the ribbon, the contained spaces.
