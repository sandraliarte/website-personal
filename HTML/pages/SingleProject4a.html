Solution

The school is located in the north of the place, being related to the street. The rest of the parcel is linked with the adjacent parks. The southern slope is conceived like an attractive playground.
As this is a place to learn, the bearing structure is in full view and the no-bearing structure, like glass or walls, is inside the bearing structure. The skeleton of the project is based on the repetition of a frame every 6m. The beam-frame has three parts: 8m (classrooms), 14m (central free space) and 8m (classrooms). The structure is naked, being glass or sandwich panel closed in between the each beam-frame. Thus, the proyectual concept is inseparable of the constructive solution.

Boxes

The school is a modulated sequence of frames, where the glass boxes burst in this modulation, creating a social dynamism. In this picture, red is the classroom (inside the modulation) and in blue is the glass-boxes, common boxes for everybody (like gym, dining hall, changing rooms...), so common glass boxes make the space more interactive and dynamic.

Beam Solution

After structural calculation in CYPE, we needed an IPE 20x80cm for the central space. Thus, we have created a special beam, joining two C-profiles. So, we increased inertia and we reduce the height of the beam (the largest 50cm high and 30cm small). We also have an area of overlap between the profiles in C (10cm) to incorporate within it the glass and sandwich panels, for hidden metalworks. Inside this 10cm we also put insulation in order to prevent thermal bridges.
